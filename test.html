MANIFESTO

Coding can often feel like magic. It is implementing your thoughts as action, even if that action requires speeds or abilities that are not humanly possible. Programming is exhilarating, but it is also powerful. It is a power that can amplify any realm it glances and make it electric and alive.

I aim to apply this electricity to public economics. As the economy ebbs and flows from its origin, unfurling itself linearly, it reflects the social norms and cultural shifts that become inherent to it. As a result, groups of people find themselves historically victimized by a system that wasn’t designed for their participation. Barriers to access in education, health, and labor persist today, and each reinforces the next.

The economy is the house built by a shortsighted architect, and coding is the floral toolbox of wireless power tools that can make it a home to all its residents. A web application can help standardize education or rework a firm’s hiring processes, and the result is voltaic. Suddenly, suppliers can augment their talent pools, and laborers can enjoy equal wages and employment opportunities. Renovations are possible today, at the crux of Computer Science and Economics. Let’s level the playing field.
